Research

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the Earth Sciences Department at the University of Southern California and a Graduate Student Intern at NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory. My work utilizes a variety of platforms accompanied by extensive field campaigns to link surface remote detection of heavy metals in plants and surface mineral spectroscopy to subsurface processes. I am also contributing to the EMIT mission, which is tasked with understanding what role mineral dust has in Earth's engery budget. My work uses combined thermal infrared and imaging spectroscopy data to remotely detect global distributions of the two most common minerals on Earth's surface, quartz and feldspar, the two minerals EMIT cannot 'see'.
